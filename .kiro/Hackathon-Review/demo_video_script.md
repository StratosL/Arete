## Part 1: Research Synthesis

### Key Psychological Principles Applied

#### The 3-Second Rule
Research shows 65% of viewers who watch the first 3 seconds will continue for 10+ seconds. Our opening must deliver immediate value.

**Source:** [Buffer - Good Hooks](https://buffer.com/resources/good-hooks/)

#### AIDA Framework
- **Attention** (0-5 sec): Pattern interrupt + problem statement
- **Interest** (5-45 sec): Show the pain, introduce solution
- **Desire** (45-180 sec): Demonstrate transformation, social proof
- **Action** (180-210 sec): Clear CTA with reduced friction

**Source:** [HubSpot - AIDA Model](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/aida-model)

#### Hero's Journey Positioning
The viewer (job seeker) is the hero. Arete is the guide/mentor that helps them succeed. Never position the product as the hero.

**Source:** [Emulent - Hero's Journey Marketing](https://emulent.com/blog/heros-journey-positioning-your-customers-as-the-protagonists-of-your-brand-story/)

#### Cognitive Biases Leveraged
1. **Loss Aversion**: "Stop losing opportunities to ATS filters"
2. **Social Proof**: "Join thousands of tech professionals"
3. **Scarcity/Urgency**: "Every application without optimization is a missed opportunity"

**Source:** [WiserNotify - Loss Aversion Marketing](https://wisernotify.com/blog/loss-aversion-marketing/)

#### Y Combinator Clarity Principle
"Can you describe your company in two sentences that anyone could understand?"

**Arete's 2-Sentence Pitch:**
> "Arete is an AI-powered resume optimizer built specifically for tech professionals. It understands the difference between React and Angular, quantifies your GitHub impact, and optimizes your resume in real-time with streaming AI feedback."

**Source:** [Y Combinator - Pitch Deck Guide](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/2u-how-to-build-your-seed-round-pitch-deck)

## Part 2: Video Structure

### Optimal Timing (3:30 Total)

| Section | Duration | Purpose | Psychological Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------------------|
| Hook | 0:00-0:05 | Pattern interrupt | Novelty detection |
| Problem | 0:05-0:25 | Establish pain | Loss aversion |
| Solution Intro | 0:25-0:40 | Promise transformation | Curiosity gap |
| Demo: Upload | 0:40-1:10 | Show simplicity | Competence signal |
| Demo: GitHub | 1:10-1:35 | Unique differentiator | Social proof |
| Demo: Job Analysis | 1:35-2:00 | Address workflow | Value-first |
| Demo: AI Optimization | 2:00-2:40 | **Visual highlight** | Emotional peak |
| Demo: Export | 2:40-3:00 | Complete journey | Closure need |
| CTA + Tech Stack | 3:00-3:30 | Drive action | Authority + urgency |

### Pacing Guidelines

Based on research from [Sound Images](https://soundimages.net.au/blog/mastering-video-length-and-pacing/):

- **Every 3-5 seconds**: Visual change (new screen, zoom, highlight)
- **Every 15-20 seconds**: Transition to new concept
- **At 2:00 mark**: "Burst sequence" with faster cuts during SSE streaming
- **Vary rhythm**: Slow for important points, fast for transitions

## Part 4: Visual Direction

### Screen Recording Guidelines

1. **Clean Browser**: Remove bookmarks bar, use incognito mode
2. **Cursor Highlighting**: Use cursor spotlight effect
3. **Zoom on Actions**: 1.5x zoom when clicking important buttons
4. **Smooth Scrolling**: Avoid jerky movements
5. **Resolution**: 1920x1080 minimum, 4K preferred

## Part 5: Audio Direction

### Narration Style

Based on research from [Narration Box](https://narrationbox.com/blog/top-ai-voices-for-product-demos/):

- **Tone**: Conversational yet authoritative (B2B SaaS standard)
- **Pace**: 150-160 words per minute (slightly faster than normal speech)
- **Energy**: Higher during demo highlights, measured during problem statement
- **Pauses**: 0.5 second pause before key features

### Background Music

- **Style**: Minimal electronic, low-key corporate
- **Volume**: -20dB below narration
- **Transitions**: Subtle swell during "AI Optimization" section
- **Recommendation**: Artlist or Epidemic Sound "Tech/Corporate" category

### Sound Effects (Subtle)

- File drop: Soft "whoosh"
- Data appearing: Gentle "pop"
- Success states: Subtle "ding"
- Avoid: Aggressive transitions, gaming sounds
